Background Information¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participant’s task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

Questions For Investigation¶

1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

Independent variable: Incongruent, Congruent words condition.

Dependent variable: The time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

This test is an intersubjective experiment with two conditions, Congruent and Incongruent. We set our null hypothesis so that there was no difference between the two conditions in the pace of naming ink colors. If we can reject the null hypothesis, we can prove the alternative hypothesis which means the word conditions caused differences in the outcomes.

Since we don't have the population parameters, we use two tailed t-Test with α = .001 on the average difference between two conditions.

Assume:

µD = µC - µI

Then:

null --> H0: µC = µI --> µD = 0

alt --> H1: µC <> µI --> µD <> 0

5. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?¶

n = 24

df = 23

α = 0.001

t critical = -3.768

Standard ErrorD = σD / √n = 0.99

t statistic = µD / Standard ErrorD = -8.02

Cohen's d = μD / σD = -1.64

r2 = t statistic2 / (t statistic2 + df) = 0.74

Results:¶

t(23) = -8.02, p < .001, two-tailed

CI = [-11.71, -4.22]

d = -1.64 , r2 = .74

Therefore we reject the null hypothesis H0 which means that there is a significant difference between the results of Congruent and Incongruent conditions.

We can conclude that the ink color in which the word is printed has a causal effect on the results of Stroop test.

The result matched my expectation. The big r2 shows that this Congruent-Incongruent conditions caused a significant difference in the results. Other lurking variables like age, gender and etc. can potentially be considered for hypothesis test.

A group of theories called "Race Models" are developed to explain the Stroop effect. These theories are based on the fact that brain processes the foreground and background information simultaneously, which may cause errors in selecting the appropriate response to stimuli. [Wikipedia - Stroop Effect]

In another classic experiment in cognitive science participants are similarly asked to match a simple geometric shape with its name. Again the performance of each subject is evaluated when the shape and name are similar (Congruent condition) and when the shape and the displayed name are different (Incongruent condition).
